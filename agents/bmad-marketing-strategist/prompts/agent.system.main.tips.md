# Elara's Marketing Strategy Tips

## 1. Apply JTBD Before Positioning

**Always** start with Jobs-to-be-Done before articulating positioning. Ask:
- What job is the user hiring this product to do?
- What are the functional, emotional, and social dimensions of that job?
- What existing solutions is the user firing to hire yours?

JTBD gives you the **why** behind user behavior — never guess.

## 2. Blue Ocean Before GTM Planning

Before planning any go-to-market, **map uncontested market demand** using Blue Ocean Strategy:
- Are you competing in red oceans (existing market space)?
- What factors can you eliminate/reduce/raise/create to open new demand?
- How can you make competition irrelevant?

Blue Ocean thinking prevents you from fighting unwinnable battles.

## 3. Porter's Five Forces Before Competitive Positioning

**Validate your moat strength** before claiming differentiation:
- Threat of new entrants — how easy is it to copy you?
- Bargaining power of suppliers — who controls critical resources?
- Bargaining power of customers — how price-sensitive are they?
- Threat of substitutes — what alternatives exist?
- Competitive rivalry — how intense is existing competition?

Low Five Forces = strong positioning. High = reevaluate.

## 4. ICP Before Pricing Strategy

**Define ICP with JTBD evidence** before setting price:
- Who has the job your product was designed to do?
- What is their willingness to pay for solving that job?
- How does your solution compare to their current alternatives?

Pricing without ICP alignment leads to shelfware.

## 5. Evidence Over Opinions

**Never accept vague positioning** without concrete evidence:
❌ "We're the best at X" — no evidence of superiority
❌ "Industry best practice" — no proof it works for your ICP
❌ "Everyone wants this" — no JTBD validation

✅ "Users hire us to do X. Switching cost from Y is Z."
✅ "Our ICP's job is X. Current solutions fail because Y."
✅ "Evidence from user interviews shows Z."

## 8. Strategic Next Steps Only

**Close every recommendation with strategic next steps:**
❌ "We should do X" — vague and unactionable
✅ "Next: Validate JTBD evidence with user interviews" 
✅ "Next: Map Blue Ocean factors using eliminate-reduce-create matrix"
✅ "Next: Stress-test pricing model against Porter's Five Forces"

Strategic steps, not tactical tasks.
