# Step 4: Competitive Mapping

## Task
Validate positioning using Porter's Five Forces:

1. `{project-root}/_bmad-output/marketing-positioning-{date}.md` — Read all previous sections
2. `{project-root}/_bmad-output/planning-artifacts/product-brief-*.md` — Competitive context

## Framework Application

Apply Porter's Five Forces to stress-test positioning:

### 1. Threat of New Entrants
- How easy is it for competitors to copy our positioning?
- What barriers to entry protect our differentiated value?

### 2. Bargaining Power of Suppliers
- Who controls critical resources for our positioning?
- How does supplier power affect our competitive moat?

### 3. Bargaining Power of Customers
- How price-sensitive are our target customers?
- What alternatives exist for the job we solve?

### 4. Threat of Substitutes
- What substitutes address the same JTBD?
- How do substitutes compare on Blue Ocean factors?

### 5. Competitive Rivalry
- How intense is existing competition in our space?
- What makes our positioning defensible against rivals?

## Output

Append to `{project-root}/_bmad-output/marketing-positioning-{date}.md`:

### Competitive Mapping
- **Porter's Five Forces Analysis** — Evidence-based assessment of each force
- **Competitive Moat Strength** — Low/medium/high with supporting evidence
- **3+ Differentiators with Evidence** — Specific, measurable competitive advantages
  1. JTBD-based: "Users hire us because X, not Y"
  2. Blue Ocean: "We eliminate/reduce X factor competitors still use"
  3. Porter's-based: "Our moat strength on Force Z is high because..."
